Johannes Tinctoris (c1430-1511) was a Brabantine musical theorist and pedagogical writer. He is regarded as one of the most significant writers of his day on music and notation, and was a wide-ranging practitioner as composer, singer and instrumentalist. These pages gather together materials relating to his theoretical works and include editions, translations, images and commentaries.
