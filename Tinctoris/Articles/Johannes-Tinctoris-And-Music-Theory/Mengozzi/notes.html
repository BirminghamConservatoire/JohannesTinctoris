As an editor, Woodley argues, Tinctoris at least at times honors the authority of his musical texts even when they flatly contradict his own authority as a theorist, implicitly admitting that the ‘rhetorical registers’ of music theory (by which the elements of music are parsed, described, explained, and argued about) are not necessarily attuned to the pragmatic choices that often govern musical behaviors. Tinctoris’s own pragmatic attitude about the applicability of his precepts, one may add, points to his awareness of the rhetorical nature of music theory.
