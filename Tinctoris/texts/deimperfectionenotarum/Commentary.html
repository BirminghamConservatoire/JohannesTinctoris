Bars  
10–12 This L cannot imperfect the preceding Mx since it is ligated with the following Mx, and the whole vale of the ligature obviously cannot imperfect the Mx at bars 1–9. Rather, this L imperfects the following Mx a parte ante.
22 This first ligated B cannot imperfect the preceding L, since again the whole value of the ligature cannot accomplish this, i.e. the same argument as bars 10–12 at the next mensural level down.
25–7 Compound imperfection a parte ante: the S imperfects the following B, and this B-equivalent group then imperfects the following L.
29–30 Second of two B altered before following L (perfect minor modus).
31–3 This L can be imperfected by the whole value of the following ligature (two S: hence imperfection quantum ad partes propinquas). Because another perfect L follows this ligature, the ligated semibreves could in principle imperfect this a parte ante, but according to the earlier part of Tinctoris’s Ninth General Rule imperfection a parte post must take priority in these circumstances.
37 The two ligated S cannot imperfect the preceding L (by analogy with bar 33), since they are further ligated to a following L and the whole value of the ligature cannot accomplish the imperfection a parte post. The semibreves must therefore be counted in with the following ligated L, imperfecting it a parte ante, quantum ad partes propinquas.
40 The initial ligated B could in principle imperfect the preceding L further quantum ad totum, but is prevented from doing so by its ligation to two other following breves, where the total value of the ligature again obviously exceeds that which is able to accomplish the imperfection.
